Overview

Purpose

Make a project site that can display stuff I am working on on various pages. Some pages may be small games in js, others might be a gallery of videos or images. Just a beginning exploration into making something useful to put on the web. My personal goal is to emulate janky websites from the old web days where it was just some dude making a blog with awful colors and flashy lights. The value add for this website will be basically nothing other than what is shown on the face. I want visitors to the sit to be left with a small moment of joy or something silly like that. No information will be necessary to interact with the site.

The first page will be a test page describing practice with CSS and showing a few ways to do so.

The second page will be a little birthday page with pictures, a comment board, a video embed, and a full page background.

Audience

The audience for this website is going to be pretty much just me and my friends.

Dynamic elements

The dynamic elements of my page that involve JS will include the nav menu, a dropdown menu for smaller screens, and in the mspaint project, most of the function will be from the js entirely. Drawing, ereasing, saving the drawing, and so on are all dynamic from the JS.

Drawing Board Content

Browser-Based Painting!

The purpose of this page is to demonstrate the ability to make dynamic painting possible in browsers using just JS and some tricky code. Feel free to experiment, draw, save, and do whatever you like with this proof-of-concept.

Note: The paint bucket tool was planned for implementation, but when I tried to get the math to work by replacing every pixel that failed a specific check, I rapidly ran into a browser crashing bug. Turns out, doing tons and tons of math to check every adjacent pixel and replace it with the selected color is not the proper way to implement this feature! I figured it wasn't super important and left it behind as an artifact of failed effort.
